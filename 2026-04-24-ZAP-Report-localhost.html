          Alert description

          The Anti-MIME-Sniffing header X-Content-Type-Options was not set to 'nosniff'. This allows older versions of Internet Explorer and Chrome to perform MIME-sniffing on the response body, potentially causing the response body to be interpreted and displayed as a content type other than the declared content type. Current (early 2014) and legacy versions of Firefox will use the declared content type (if one is set), rather than performing MIME-sniffing.
          Other info

          This issue still applies to error type pages (401, 403, 500, etc.) as those pages are often still affected by injection issues, in which case there is still concern for browsers sniffing pages away from their actual content type.

          At "High" threshold this scan rule will not alert on client or server error responses.

**Key Elements of an NDA:**

1. **Confidential Information**: This refers to the sensitive information that the disclosing party wants to keep confidential.
2. **Recipient**: The person or entity receiving the confidential information.
3. **Obligations**: The recipient agrees not to disclose the confidential information to anyone else.
4. **Duration**: The length of time the recipient is bound by the NDA.
5. **Penalties**: Consequences for breaching the NDA.

1. **Financial Penalties**: The recipient may be liable for damages, fines, or other financial penalties for breaching the NDA.
2. **Loss of Reputation**: Breaching an NDA can damage the recipient's reputation and credibility.
3. **Lawsuits**: The disclosing party may sue the recipient for breach of contract.

**What is a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)?**

3. **Non-Use**: The receiving party agrees not to use the confidential information for their own benefit or to compete with the disclosing party.
4. **Term**: The NDA specifies the duration of the agreement, which can be a specific period or ongoing.
